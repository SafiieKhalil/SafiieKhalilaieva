Reading Reflection #3

Before reading this graphic novel, I thought of motherhood mostly as a happy thing—smiling babies, family hugs, and a mother who always knows what to do. But this story showed me a completely different side. It made me understand that for many women, being a mother can also be a silent struggle.

The book uses pictures and words together to show a mother who is deeply tired, not just in her body but in her heart and mind. We see her getting up at night, making food, cleaning, and taking care of everyone, but no one takes care of her. The drawings are powerful because you can see the tiredness in her eyes and the heavy weight on her shoulders. The art is simple but very emotional. It shows how she often feels invisible.

One of the main points I understood is the feeling of loneliness. The mother in the story is surrounded by people—her children, her partner, maybe her family—but she is still alone. No one asks how she feels. No one notices that she is breaking inside. The graphic novel calls this a “crisis.” It is not a crisis like a loud accident. It is a quiet crisis that happens slowly, day after day, until the mother feels she has lost herself. She used to have dreams, friends, and a life of her own. Now, she only exists for others.

The story also shows the pressure from society. People expect mothers to be perfect, patient, and always happy. If a mother says she is struggling, people might judge her or say she is not grateful. This book fights that idea. It says it is normal to feel lost, angry, and sad. Motherhood is hard work, and no one can do it alone.

What touched me the most was the ending. It does not give an easy happy answer. Instead, it shows that the first step to solving the crisis is to speak up. The mother finally admits she needs help. She is not a bad mother for feeling this way—she is human.

After reading, I feel more respect for all mothers. I learned that we should not just say “thank you” to our moms. We should look at them, listen to them, and ask if they are okay. This graphic novel is small but very strong. It opened my eyes to a truth that many people ignore: a mother’s mental health matters. Without support, motherhood can become a crisis. With support, it can become what it should be—a shared journey, not a lonely battle.
